The Flushed University Rankings

Which university will leave you flushed with success?

We’ve undertaken a cisternmatic review of universities’ wastewater by comparing it to their staff and student head counts (HESA, 2018/19).

Wastewater is any water that has been contaminated by human use (in this case, from university owned estates), and is calculated by meter readings or utility company estimates [source].

To make the data more meaningful to applicants, we’ve got our hands dirty and calculated the equivalent number of toilet flushes per member of staff or student per year. According to Southern Water, one cubic metre = 11 baths or 31 showers or 116 toilet flushes.

The rankings do not take into account The Open University's* students’ home toilet use (FOI pending), upper management dribble, or leaking pipes. It also doesn’t take into account how much universities “massage” their data before submitting it to HESA.

I am obviously not a data expert. If you would like to submit constructive feedback about my methodology, please print your letter of complaint and shred it immediately. Pro tip: on mobile, hold your phone horizontally to see all table columns.
