The University Bike Rack Rankings

Another wheelie useful ranking from @BantshireUni

Take a look at this wheelie useful, be-spoke data before the next recruitment cycle.

We've ranked universities based on their unused bike spaces.

First, we calculated the total number of staff and students who commute by bike per university, by using the percentage modal split for commuting data from HESA. We then compared this to the total number of cycle spaces at HE providers (again, HESA). We’ve removed any universities who did not have data available for any of the columns required.

We've ordered the table so that universities with the most number of available cycle spaces are top. Mainly because we wanted to see Oxford rank last. We've also assumed that all cyclists have ridden to campus at exactly the same time.

Honourable mention to Plymouth College of Art for their finely balanced cycle space provision. And before you ask, no, the Open University don't win because "students can park their bikes anywhere".

All of these rankings have made our data expert wheelie, wheelie tyred. Cheers.
